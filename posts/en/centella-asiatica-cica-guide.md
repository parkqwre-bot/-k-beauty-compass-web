## What is Centella Asiatica (Cica)?

In the world of K-Beauty, one ingredient reigns supreme for calming sensitive and irritated skin: **Centella Asiatica**, often called **Cica**. Also known as Tiger Grass, legend has it that tigers roll in this plant to heal their wounds. Today, it’s a scientifically backed powerhouse for skin repair.

As environmental stressors and active ingredients like Retinol become more common in 2026 routines, Cica has evolved from a trend to a staple. It is the ultimate "rescue" ingredient.

## Why Cica is Essential in 2026

The focus of 2026 skincare is "Barrier Repair" and "Inflammaging" (aging caused by inflammation). Cica directly addresses both concerns.

### Key Benefits:
1.  **Instant Soothing:** Calms redness, irritation, and inflammation almost immediately.
2.  **Barrier Repair:** Stimulates collagen synthesis and strengthens the skin barrier.
3.  **Acne Healing:** Its antibacterial properties help treat acne without drying out the skin.
4.  **Hydration:** Rich in amino acids and fatty acids, it deeply hydrates while soothing.

## How to Incorporate Cica into Your Routine

Cica is incredibly versatile and can be used morning and night. It pairs well with almost all active ingredients, often buffering their potential irritation.

*   **Morning:** Use a Cica toner or ampoule to calm morning redness and prep the skin. A Cica-based sunscreen can also provide extra soothing protection.
*   **Evening:** After using exfoliating acids or Retinol, apply a thick layer of Cica cream or sleeping mask to repair the skin barrier overnight.
*   **Emergency Care:** Use a Cica sheet mask or spot treatment on inflamed acne or irritated patches for quick relief.
