### Why Does Sunscreen Make Your Eyes Sting?

The primary culprits behind eye stinging are certain chemical UV filters commonly found in Western sunscreens, such as **Avobenzone, Oxybenzone, and Octocrylene**. These ingredients can be irritating if they migrate into the eyes through sweat or natural skin oils. Additionally, fragrances and certain preservatives can contribute to the discomfort.

### The K-Beauty Advantage: Modern UV Filters

Korean sunscreens are world-renowned because they often utilize the latest generation of UV filters that are not yet approved in some other regions (like the US). Filters such as **Tinosorb S, Tinosorb M, Uvinul A Plus, and Uvinul T 150** are larger molecules that are less likely to penetrate the skin or migrate into the eyes, making them much gentler.
