If you've been following K-Beauty trends, you've likely seen **Mugwort** (or *Artemisia*) everywhere. Often referred to as the "Korean Tea Tree," this resilient herb has been used for centuries in traditional Korean medicine (Hanbang) for its healing and detoxifying properties.

In recent years, it has become a superstar ingredient in skincare, especially for those with sensitive, acne-prone, or redness-prone skin.

### What is Mugwort?
Mugwort is a hardy perennial herb that grows abundantly in Korea. In skincare, the most prized variety is *Artemisia Princeps*, often harvested from Ganghwa Island, known for its clean environment and sea breezes that enhance the plant's potency.

### Why Your Skin Needs Mugwort
1. **Intense Soothing:** Mugwort is naturally anti-inflammatory. It effectively calms redness, irritation, and skin sensitivity almost instantly.
2. **Antibacterial and Antifungal:** It helps fight acne-causing bacteria and prevents future breakouts without drying out the skin.
3. **Rich in Antioxidants:** Packed with Vitamin A and C, it protects the skin from environmental stressors and helps brighten the complexion.
4. **Skin Barrier Repair:** It helps to strengthen the skin's natural barrier, making it more resilient against external irritants.

### How to Use Mugwort in Your Routine
The best way to experience Mugwort is through **Essences** or **Wash-off Masks**.
*   **Essences:** A single-ingredient Mugwort essence (like the famous ones from I'm From or Missha) can be used as a first step after cleansing to prep and calm the skin.
*   **Masks:** A Mugwort clay mask or cream mask is perfect for a "reset" when your skin feels overheated or stressed.
