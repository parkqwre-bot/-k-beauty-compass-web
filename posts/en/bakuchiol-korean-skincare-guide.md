## What is Bakuchiol?

For decades, Retinol has been the gold standard for anti-aging. However, its tendency to cause redness, peeling, and sensitivity—especially for those with reactive skin—has led many to seek gentler alternatives. Enter **Bakuchiol**, a plant-derived antioxidant extracted from the seeds and leaves of the *Psoralea corylifolia* (Babchi) plant.

In 2026, Bakuchiol has officially become a K-Beauty staple. It offers many of the same benefits as Retinol—such as stimulating collagen production and speeding up cell turnover—without the harsh side effects.

## Why Bakuchiol is Trending in K-Beauty

The Korean philosophy of "skin first" focuses on maintaining a healthy barrier while achieving results. Bakuchiol aligns perfectly with this approach.

### Key Benefits:
1.  **Gentle Anti-Aging:** Reduces the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles without the typical "Retinol burn."
2.  **Safe for Sensitive Skin:** Unlike Retinol, Bakuchiol is stable and doesn't cause irritation or dryness, making it ideal for those who previously couldn't use anti-aging actives.
3.  **Day & Night Use:** Retinol is sensitive to sunlight and can only be used at night. Bakuchiol is photostable, meaning you can safely apply it in your morning routine.
4.  **Brightening:** It helps even out skin tone and reduce hyperpigmentation, contributing to the coveted "Glass Skin" look.

## How to Use Bakuchiol in Your Routine

One of the best things about Bakuchiol is how easy it is to incorporate into your existing regimen.

*   **When to Apply:** You can use it twice a day—after cleansing and toning, but before your moisturizer.
*   **Layering:** Bakuchiol plays well with others. You can safely layer it with Vitamin C, Niacinamide, and Hyaluronic Acid.
*   **Sun Protection:** While Bakuchiol isn't photosensitive, you should always follow up with a broad-spectrum sunscreen in the morning to protect your skin's progress.
