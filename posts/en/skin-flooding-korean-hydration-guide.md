If you've been following K-beauty trends lately, you might have heard of a technique called **"Skin Flooding."** While the name sounds intense, it is actually one of the gentlest and most effective ways to achieve deep, lasting hydration and that radiant "glass skin" look.

In this guide, we’ll dive into what skin flooding is, why it’s a game-changer for dry and dehydrated skin, and how you can master this technique using your favorite K-beauty products.

### What is Skin Flooding?

Skin flooding is a specialized layering technique designed to "flood" your skin with moisture by applying multiple hydrating products on damp skin. Instead of applying one thick cream, you layer lightweight, water-based products in a specific order to ensure maximum absorption and retention.

The goal is to push moisture deep into the dermis and seal it in, preventing trans-epidermal water loss (TEWL).

### Why Skin Flooding Works

The secret lies in the science of **humectants** and **occlusives**. 

1. **Humectants** (like Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, and Panthenol) attract water molecules.
2. **Damp Skin** acts as a magnet, allowing humectants to pull that moisture into the skin more effectively.
3. **Occlusives** (like Ceramides, Squalane, and Shea Butter) create a protective barrier to lock all that moisture inside.

### Step-by-Step K-Beauty Skin Flooding Routine

To get the best results, follow these steps immediately after cleansing:

#### Step 1: Start with Damp Skin
Do not dry your face completely after washing. Leaving your skin slightly damp is the most critical part of skin flooding. If your skin is already dry, mist it with a hydrating facial spray or thermal water.

#### Step 2: Apply a Hydrating Toner or Essence
Use a lightweight, watery toner. In K-beauty, this is often where the "7-skin method" comes in—applying several thin layers of toner to build up hydration. Look for ingredients like Beta-Glucan or Birch Sap.

#### Step 3: Layer Your Serums (The "Flooding" Phase)
Apply 1-2 hydrating serums. Hyaluronic acid is the star here. Apply it while the toner is still wet on your face. You can also add a soothing serum with Centella Asiatica or Heartleaf if your skin is irritated.

#### Step 4: Seal it with a Moisturizer
While your skin still feels plump and wet from the serums, apply your moisturizer. This "seals the floodgates." For oily skin, a gel cream works best. For dry skin, look for a ceramide-rich cream to fortify your skin barrier.

### Who Should Try Skin Flooding?

Skin flooding is a miracle for those with **dry, dehydrated, or sensitive skin**. It is also excellent for repairing a damaged skin barrier. However, if you have extremely acne-prone skin, be careful with heavy occlusives and stick to non-comedogenic, oil-free layers.

Skin flooding is more than just a trend; it's a return to the core principle of K-beauty: **Hydration First.** By layering strategically on damp skin, you can transform dull, thirsty skin into a glowing, healthy complexion.
